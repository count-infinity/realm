# 065. Pet

> Checklist item 65 — [now] — *db.following, listen-lock whitelists, spoken orders*

**What you'll build:** Biscuit, a ginger hound. Say "Biscuit, heel"
and he pads after you through every exit you take; "stay" parks him;
"speak" gets a bark. Only *you* — his owner — can give orders,
enforced not by if-checks in every script but by one lock on his ears.
**Concepts:** the engine's follow system as one attribute
(`db.following`), `^listen` triggers as a voice-command surface, the
**listen lock** as an order whitelist, ownership as the authority
line.

## How it works

**Following is one attribute.** The engine's whole follow system is
`db.following = <leader id>`: when a leader walks through an exit,
everything in the room whose `following` names them walks after
(blocked, unconscious, or fighting followers stay behind — they take
the exit for real, locks and guards included). Players get it via the
`follow` command; *anything else can opt in by writing the attribute*.
A pet is an NPC whose scripts set `following` on itself — no leash
code, no movement loop. `party` even shows him in your column.

**Orders are overheard speech.** Each trick is a `^listen` trigger —
`^*heel*`, `^*stay*`, `^*speak*` — so orders are just talking:
"Biscuit, heel", "HEEL, damn you", "would you heel" all land. The
scripts run *as Biscuit* (mutating only himself: his `following`, his
poses), so a shouted order is never a capability leak.

**The whitelist is a lock, not an if.** Anyone in the room can say
"heel". The engine gates whose speech may fire an object's listen
triggers with the **listen lock** — one line:

```
@lock/listen Biscuit = caller.id == owner.id
```

`owner` in a lock expression is the object's owner — you, who
`@create`d him. Every `^pattern` on Biscuit now ignores everyone else,
current and future tricks alike. (Compare item 64's Mira, whose
listens are deliberately public.) One dog, one line of policy, zero
per-trick checks.

## Build it

A yard, and the dog (from your workroom — `@create` puts him in your
hands, `drop` sets him loose):

```
@dig The Kennel Yard = yard, out
yard
@create Biscuit
@tag Biscuit = npc
drop Biscuit
@desc Biscuit = A ginger hound with an opinion about everything.
@lock/listen Biscuit = caller.id == owner.id
@set Biscuit/listen_heel = ^*heel*:set_attr(me, 'following', enactor.id); pose(f"falls in at {name(enactor)}'s heel.")
@set Biscuit/listen_stay = ^*stay*:del_attr(me, 'following'); pose('sits, ears up, exactly where told.')
@set Biscuit/listen_speak = ^*speak*:pose('barks once, sharp and proud.')
```

That's the entire pet: three overheard words and a lock.

## Try it

```
say Biscuit, speak      → Biscuit barks once, sharp and proud.
say Biscuit, heel       → Biscuit falls in at your heel.
out                     → he follows you out (walk anywhere — stairs,
                          gates, guarded doors: he takes the real exits,
                          and what blocks you blocks him)
party                   → Biscuit — following you
```

Now the whitelist. Have a friend try to poach him:

```
(friend) say Biscuit, stay    → nothing. Not a twitch.
(you)    say Biscuit, stay    → Biscuit sits, ears up, exactly where told.
out                           → you leave; Biscuit stays put
```

`@examine Biscuit` any time: `following` is there when he's yours to
walk, gone when he's told to stay — the whole system in one attribute.

## Going further

- **Fetch:** `^*fetch*:cmd('get ' + arg0)` — the scripted `get` routes
  through the real verb, so he can't fetch what's locked down. Bound
  wildcards (`arg0`) carry what was said.
- **Feeding and mood:** an `ON_RECEIVE` hook (item 62's offering
  pattern) that `adjust_disposition(me, enactor, 1)` — and gate the
  tricks on `disposition(me, enactor)`: a neglected dog stops
  listening.
- **A guard dog:** compose with item 62 — `@behavior Biscuit =
  aggressive, target_tags:["burglar"]` and he's a pet *and* a defense,
  with `spare_at` sparing the family.
- **Transferable leash:** replace the lock with
  `caller.id == get_attr(target, 'trusted', '')` and a `$entrust *`
  command that writes the attribute — a pet you can lend.
